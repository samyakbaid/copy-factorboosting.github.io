Financial Factor Data Library


This website is dedicated to the Fama-French Factors for the Indian equity markets. The explanatory power of Fama-French factors in capturing stock returns variation is well established in the literature. Several data repositories exist globally, and a handful cover Indian markets. We drew inspiration from these existing resources. What we set out to do differently is to build a resource that is clean, investible, reproducible, and India-aware. We have documented our construction process with full transparency. This will allow practitioners/researchers to replicate, interrogate, and build on our work with confidence. Our coverage includes the Fama-French factors: Value, Size, Investment, and Operating Profitability along with Momentum, for firms listed on both the NSE and BSE, starting April 2002 and updated monthly. Our data source is the CMIE Prowess.


Key contributions of our work:

  • Beyond aggregate factor returns, we also provide monthly stock-level factor labels. This allows users to construct their own portfolios from the ground up, audit factor exposures in existing holdings, and study cross-sectional variation across the market.
  • The library includes an interactive analysis tool that lets users visualize factor and portfolio returns, backtest custom strategies across different factor combinations, and compare multiple portfolios side by side with detailed performance metrics and stock level holdings.
  • We have applied rigorously defined filters and market-structure adjustments throughout. This makes sure that only tradable stocks are included and the time series is not distorted by outliers. Refer to our methodology for details.
  • Factors will be available across three universes: top 300, top 500 (coming soon), and the full tradable universe — giving users the flexibility to calibrate exposure to their own liquidity and risk preferences. All datasets are available in the Downloads section. [Try Interactive Tool]
  • Unlike the existing resources, every breakpoint used in portfolio construction is empirically grounded in the structure of the Indian market. Full documentation is available here.

The Centre is actively exploring what comes next. We are working towards bringing together factor frameworks with the frontier of machine learning. Our current work involves integrating Fama-French factors with modern deep learning architectures for mutual fund holdings analysis and portfolio optimization. We are also investigating Transaction Cost modelling under real-world constraints. We will update the website with additional features in the near future.

Data Notes

  • All returns are in percentage form
  • Missing values appear as blank cells (NaN when loaded into pandas)
  • Data is updated monthly, typically on the 5th business day
  • For detailed variable definitions, see our documentation
  • Please cite the appropriate papers when using this data in research

About

Rapid advancements in data, compute, and algorithms are profoundly transforming our world, and we’re still in the early stages of this data science, machine learning, and AI revolution.

At SCDLDS, we conduct high-level applied and methodological research in data and learning sciences to aid in informed decision-making and address complex societal challenges. Initially, our work is focused on four key areas: ⁠Data-driven financial research, ⁠Weather and climate modeling, Reinforcement learning, and Epidemiological modeling.

In addition to our research, we are committed to teaching. We train young minds through engaging coursework and projects, exposing them to a deep and varied mathematical toolkit that helps them uncover hidden patterns in large and complex datasets.
